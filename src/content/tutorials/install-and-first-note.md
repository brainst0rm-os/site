## What you'll do

In five minutes you'll install Brainstorm, create your first vault, open the Notes app, and write a note that's saved to a file on your disk.

## What you'll need

- A Mac (macOS 13 or later), Windows 11, or a Linux desktop.
- Five minutes.
- That's it. No account.

## 1. Download

Pick your platform from [the download page](/download/mac). The shell installer is around 90 MB. The installer is signed; it should open without an "unidentified developer" warning.

## 2. Launch and pick a vault folder

Open Brainstorm. On first launch it asks where you want your vault — a folder on your disk where every document and asset lives. Pick somewhere you'll back up: `~/Documents/Brainstorm`, `~/iCloud Drive/Brainstorm`, or anywhere else.

You can have more than one vault. You can move a vault later. You can copy a vault to another machine and open it there.

## 3. Open Notes

The dashboard shows a wallpaper and a small set of icons. Click **Notes**. The app opens in its own window.

## 4. Write something

Click **New note**. Start typing. Write a paragraph about why you tried Brainstorm.

The note is saved to a file in your vault as you type. Open the vault folder in Finder / Explorer / your file manager — your note is there, as a real file, named something readable.

## 5. Close the app

Quit Brainstorm. Open the file in any other tool that can read its format. Your data is yours.
